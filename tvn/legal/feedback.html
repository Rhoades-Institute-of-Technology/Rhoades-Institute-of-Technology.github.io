Licensing Feedback

TVN Bridge's shared source licensing philosophy is a living draft. We're asking the community to help shape it. Select any section below to suggest an edit or leave a comment.

Read the full licensing philosophy

Security by Design

Why publishing the enforcement engine unrestricted creates a roadmap for circumvention.

Preventing Weaponization

Why a shared source model preserves the ability to deny access to bad actors.

Sustainable Development

Multi-tier licensing: free for civic use, paid for commercial use.

Access Through Agreement

Core values: do no harm, respect privacy, contribute back, be honest.

Tiered Licensing

Civic/educational (free), individual dev (free/low), commercial (paid), strategic partner (custom).

Democratic Governance

Open specifications, controlled reference implementation, advisory council.

Why Not AGPL?

AGPL allows any use including harmful, can't deny bad actors, enterprise compatibility issues.

Why Not Fully Proprietary?

Conflicts with civic mission, prevents community engagement, limits adoption.

The Security Standard Argument

Governed open access: source visible to vetted participants, specs open to all.
